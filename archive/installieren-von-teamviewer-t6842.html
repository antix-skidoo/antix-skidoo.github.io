    I-to-1 Assistance works very well on modern and ancient hardware. It was tested successfully (and still works) on a laptop manufactured approx 1997.

    Your post reveals your English is quite good, so read the user guide. You will be able to take that at your own pace rather than trying to understand the quickly spoken words in the video.

    Also there is a copy of the guide in the antiX-FAQ on your machine
    Help-->antiX--antiX-FAQ

    If you want to speak with the other person while you are sharing their desktop, have a look at 1-to1 Voice. It is a companion application to 1-to-1 Assistance. A user guide is also available for it.

    The easiest way to use them is where both systems are running antiX. This lets 1-to-1 do all the hard work for you (setting up the security, handling the certificates, adjusting the screen size, etc).

    It is possible to set up a connection with a non antiX system, providing it has all the software which underpins 1-to-1. Of course you then have to take care of configuring everything yourself manually. That might be quite complicated to do depending on the level of expertise of both the user and you. It is not something I am willing to become involved in.

    If both systems are running antiX, it is very easy to run 1-to-1 Assistance and 1-to-1 Voice and talk the other person through the changes you make to their system, all in real time.
