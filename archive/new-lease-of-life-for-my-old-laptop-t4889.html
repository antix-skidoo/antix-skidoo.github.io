IMO, this is the biggest"selling point" of lightweight distros like antiX -- the ability to keep old hardware usable. I'm running antiX on three systems regularly (two constant, one part time): a 1997 vintage Pentium II laptop, a ca. 2002 Athlon XP 2000+ tower, and a Core2Quad Q9400, 2.67 GHz with 4 GiB RAM and more than a terabyte of storage. It runs extremely well on the newer hadware, and keeps the old (especially the laptop) useful enough to be worth dealing with.
