I bought a Samsung NC10 two days ago.
There was a cut in price due to the delivery of a new netbook n140, a light change in the look but the same specs.

I feel more comfortable with a 10" screen than with my eeepc 701 (7"), and with a nearly full size keyboard.
Booting 8.2 full from the live-USB, I got the same problem I had with the eeepc: the boot process hanged somewhere starting X and/or slim.
I had to use the same workaround: boot the live-USB in single mode, login root at the terminal prompt after de-activating the numlock (by chance the key Alt+F11=Numlock works), then start slim.

Mepis installer worked fine. Installed antiX on a 30 GB ext3 partition.
Wifi with wicd works out of the box.
I am listening music on streamtuner, I had to push up the controls in alsamixer, sound maybe a little low.

Most of the function keys do not work, or have erroneous bindings. I miss the brightness up and down keys. Hotkeys worked better on the eeepc. Googling around, it seems that the problem is common with Debian. Got to try to fix it.

For now I feel happy with my purchase and I will stay with antiX (I tried one month ago easypeasy - aka eeebuntu, an Ubuntu derivative for netbooks) on the eepc, but it was far too heavy (booted in 2mn on the lowspec Celeron at 600 MHz, vs. 50 s for antiX)
