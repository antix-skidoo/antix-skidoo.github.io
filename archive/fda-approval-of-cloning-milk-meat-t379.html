The issue of cloning and genetically modified food/crops is a very serious subject for discussion and I would also like to know what companies with or without government backing are doing to my food.

Being/becoming a vegetarian won't be the solution either as many foodstuffs eaten by vegetarians ie soya, various beans and pulses are now genetically modifed though often without our knowing. I wish I could remember where I found the statistics showing something like 80% of soya grown is GM (in the 'undeveloped' world) and so is a high percentage of coffe, tea and cotton.

Now while it is important for us to be aware of our diet, I think it is also important to stress that modern capitalist society has made food production not for our needs, but for maximising profit. Also our fast lifestyle and consumerist mentality has led to huge increases of obesity in the"Western" world, particularly amongst women and children. Also statistics show that social class is a factor in diet. Put simply, the higher the social class the better quality of food and less health problems as a result of this, obviously due to having greater access to fresher food and of better quality.
So the problem of food production IMO is linked to the the capitalist
'philosophy' of profit before people.
We need to turn the slogan the other way around.
