I wonder if you could make a sticky out of this post? I'm sure plenty of people feel they need to have a full-feature office suite, much as they may like minimalist OS.

In short, to get OpenOffice running on AntiX 8.2, as root do the following:

1) edit /etc/apt/apt.conf to read
APT::Cache-Limit 40000000;

2) edit /etc/apt/source.list and uncomment line
deb
========= SCRAPER REMOVED AN EMBEDDED LINK HERE ===========
url was:"http://ftp.us.debian.org/debian/"
linktext was:"http://ftp.us.debian.org/debian/"
====================================
" onclick="window.open(this.href);return false unstable main contrib non-free

3) apt-get update

4) /usr/sbin/synaptic - select Edit/Fix Broken Packages [may not be necessary, but does no harm]

5) apt-get install openoffice.org

6) undo step 2

That's it, has worked on two computers for me so far.
