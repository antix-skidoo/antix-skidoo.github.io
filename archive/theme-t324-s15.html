I think it was Thoreau (maybe Emerson) who said something like this about education:"Which 'me' do you plan to teach today, and which 'me' do you plan to ignore?" (Probably misquoting, and probably someone else, like Ginsberg).

The point is the same with wallpaper and themes; I'm a different"me" on different occasions, especially when doing different things with the computer. In fact, one of the great things about getting out of the Windows world was being exposed to so *many* other ways of doing things--and thinking about things--from other Linux-users. And, for me at least, radically different themes and wallpapers have been one way of getting jolted into other mindsets. (Yes, I'm that impressionable.)

ON THE OTHER HAND. Searching for the perfect theme is like jumping from wife to wife. You have to find the one that speaks to your soul and then grapple her to your breast with hoops of steel.

I have a tendency to change my desktop (Themes, papers, gtk etc) a lot, but i do have some preferences, even among the choices i make. __{{emoticon}}__

1: I really wish that there were fluxbox themes that looked as good as the OpenBox ones! LOL!! I know i could use OpenBox, and sometimes i do, but i really like fluxbox..... i just think that, on the whole, the OpenBox themes are a lot nicer looking. The right click menu in particular is almost always good looking (as well really functional) in OpenBox.

2: I quite like the dark, glassy type of theme. That's one of the reasons that i find TinyMe (& PCLOS in general) so attractive..... they just look really good.

3: Wallpapers - It's so personal that i think it's hard to please everyone, and i generally find that, whatever distro i'm using, i'll almost always change the wallpaper.

4: Rounded Corners - Not really fussed either way, if a theme looks good, it looks good, no matter whether the corners are rounded or squared.

Can't think of anything else at the moment, but i'm sure something else will crop up soon! __{{emoticon}}__
