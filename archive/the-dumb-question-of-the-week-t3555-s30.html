Weighing in on the original question a bit late, but I have a pretty strong opinion, based on experience in this matter. I definitely keep multiple window managers, multiple desktop managers, multiple distro instances, and multiple computers available. Just their redundancy alone, not only gives me alternatives on each system, it also gives me implicit backup for many things. In this way, the only other thing I must back up is any sensitive, important, data that I cannot get from any other source. But I'm so well covered in everything else that I don't have to do much media to media backup; I do as much of it as possible over the network, minimizing my time and cost, yet also minimizing my exposure. Stuff I really don't want others seeing on the 'net are the only things I need local, offline backup for, and there isn't all that much of that kind of thing.

So having redundancy is a GOOD thing, unless you are at 90% or more disk capacity, and in that case, it may just be more expeditious to consider additional hardware.

And also this file: called xmms.sh


Code: Select all

#!/bin/bash

#This script is free to modify and distribute.
#Use at your own risk.

#Requirement: xmms-infopipe

#User Settings here
maxcol=35
file=/tmp/xmms-info

#Grab needed information
pos=`cat $file | grep uSecPosition: | cut -d : -f2`
len=`cat $file | grep uSecTime: | cut -d : -f2`

#Count the length in characters of the title
size=`echo $title | wc -c`
#Calculate current position within that length
poscol=$(echo"$pos*$maxcol/$len" | bc)

#Printing
for (( i=0; i<$maxcol; i++ ))
do
  if [ $i -eq $poscol ]; then
    echo -n 'O'
  else
    echo -n '='
  fi
done
