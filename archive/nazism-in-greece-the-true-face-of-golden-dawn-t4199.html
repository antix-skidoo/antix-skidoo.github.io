Fortunately for us in the UK, the BNP (British National Party) has never made any headway. The UK has a first-past-the-post electoral system. There are 650 (approx) constituencies nation wide. Anyone (more or less) can stand as a candidate for a constituency, when the votes for that constituency are counted the candidate with the most votes gets the seat. We do not have proportional represenatation or second preferences or anything else.

The Liberal Party, the smallest of the three majors, have called to have this reformed for years, pointing out, quite correctly, that 15% of the National vote can be for them but they end up with only 10 seats (not 15% of 659 which would give them 97).
