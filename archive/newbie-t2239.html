WHAT'S NOT: Graphics performance is this system's glaring defect. The HP's unimpressive numbers in this area were due mainly to an integrated graphics chip that relies on the main system memory. As a result, the Pavilion 8660C ran slowly in Quake 3 Arena. In addition, the HP's Intel 810 graphics subsystem--unlike those on most PCs--couldn't render graphics in 32-bit color depth (its maximum color depth is 24 bits). The 8660C also falls short on system expansion: Adding extra cards and drives isn't easy, and the cramped interior makes adding memory and accessing the two free PCI slots and one open, externally accessible drive bay difficult
