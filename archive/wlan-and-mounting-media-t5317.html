for the wireless, you problaby need to put the name of your wireless interface in the wicd preferences. in antix 13.2, its left black by default.

1. open the terminal and type"inxi -i". this will output the name of all of your interfaces. find the wireless one (probably wlan0 or wlan1) and type that into the prefernces of wicd (found in the Internet menu).

2. You are probably using ROX as your file manager, which is the default. You can mounting with spacefm (Menus>Tools>Filers>spacefm). If that works, I suggest enabling spacefm to start in daemon mode. It will sit in the background and wait for pluggable devices to show up. The will be accessible in /media whether you use spacefm or rox. to enable that feature, open the Control Center and select"edit Icwem Settings" (second row, right button). You'll get a text editor open up will all the icewm configuration files in tabs. click on the startup tab, and find the commented line that reads"run spacefm -d to automount plug-in device..." and uncomment the startup line below (spacefm -d &). Logout and log back in and you should be automounting usb's and CD's.

3. I'm unsure what is happening here. What printer driver are you trying to install, and what is the source (repos, downloaded file, etc...) There are no cd sources enabled by default in antiX. You can check sources in synaptic in the Settings>Repositories menu item from inside synaptic.

well, that is one of the trade offs with not having a real desktop environment. the window manager setup of antix works better on old gear and is quite responsive, but you don't have those little niceties like icons appearing on the desktop.

however, there are book mark features of both rox and spacefm that make getting to those areas quicker. and I like to put an item in my top-level menu for"rox /media" that opens directly to the media folder.

If you are using the spacefm desktop options, I believe you can set spacefm to open a window automatically when a usb or cdrom is inserted.
