I now think that was just the top end of the normal charge temperature range -- it's literally been more than ten years since I've had a working battery in this machine, and about that long since I've had anything with NiMH batteries (I mostly jumped straight from NiCd to Li-ion -- and I've still got NiCd in my R/C gear, which dates back to 1987; not sure if those batteries are still good, but I can upgrade them to NiMH if they aren't).

Even if I have to watch the battery temperature during charge, this is a big step up; it lets me (for instance) haul this computer down to Starbuck's or the local library and use wifi while I sip or do research without having to search for an outlet and fool with the power supply brick. A couple hours of run time is plenty for most things I'd need to do with a portable machine -- and it might get an occasional double take if anyone notices it doesn't look like the modern super-thin, melted-looking notebooks.
