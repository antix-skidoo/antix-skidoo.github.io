I recently installed antiX 7.2 on the above machine, purchasing it for around one hundred dollars in used condition. I thought I would detail my experiences here to aid anyone considering something similar for antiX. Shuttles are SFF (Small Form Factor) computers and the SB61G2 is about the size of a small toaster.

Although I have antiX installed on three other computers, the most used was a P3 1 GHz with 192MB of RAM. Performance was great but the browsers were slow and I grew tired of the delay. I decided to upgrade processor and memory to check out the difference and thus purchased the Shuttle.

The machine is a Pentium 4 (Northwood core) 2.6 GHz with hyper threading. Also a 160GB Maxtor hard drive, Sony DVD-RW (DRU-5), 1 GB DDR Ram, and integrated audio and video. The audio is AC97...Realtek ALC650F. The integrated video is Intel Extreme Graphics 2. The Shuttle had one open AGP slot and one open PCI slot.

I scrounged through my old parts pile and found an old SoundBlaster audio card and a GeForce FX 5200 128MB video card. I kept them waiting in the wings for any audio or video problems.

antiX installed fine but even forcing 1024x768 at Grub gave me 800x600. I could force it to 1024 after antiX was up but on reboot it would revert to 800x600 again. Wanting a little more oomph on the video anyway, I finally installed the GeForce card and the video problem was solved.

The sound was sporadic as well. Sound worked on initial installation but only the left channel (speaker) worked. Next boot both speakers worked fine. Next boot no audio at all. Used alsa config and mixer with mixed results. Finally defaulted to the SoundBlaster card for solid audio.

Now that I'm up and running performance has been great. From the press of the start button to the slim log-on screen is 50 seconds. So even taking the time to type in username and password I'm booted up and running in under a minute. Iceweasel takes 6 seconds to start. Both Iceweasel and Opera are nearly instantaneous to react. I rarely use even 50% of the processor and most of the time memory use is 15% or below. I haven't tried leaving iceweasel up and running. With my old P3 computer memory use would slowly increase in that scenario until I was close to maxxing memory.

I love antiX but the smile on my face is even larger with the performance I'm enjoying now. antiX is a life saver for older P3 era machines but if you want some fun put it on something faster!
