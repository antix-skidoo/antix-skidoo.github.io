caprea wrote:Hello,my pc can't read cd's anymore.It doesn't matter if they are burned or original.If i try to open them with spacefm
You cannot mount audio cds. They don't have a file system on them to mount. That is why you were getting the error messages when you tried to mount an audio cd with spacefm. You can only play audio cds, you cannot mount them. If they fail to play now, perhaps your attempts at mounting an audio cd put the cdrom device in an unhappy state.

None of your fstab entries are valid for audio cds because fstab is all about mounting devices. Also, you might be better off with a filesystem type of"auto" instead of"udf,iso9660" but as I said, these entries don't apply to audio cds.

If spacefm or some other program is trying to auto-mount your audio cds then you could try commenting out the lines in fstab for the device you want to play audio cds on. If something is trying to auto-mount your audio cds, that could possibly prevent them from playing. You might want to take a look at the dmesg output when you try to play an audio cd without trying to mount it.

Code: Select all

sudo eject -i [on off]

eject: tried to use '/dev/cdrom' as device name but it is no block device
