    The last week I have been trying out yet another distro, Crux. It's not a very well known distro. It's not as userfriendly as AntiX, more comparable to Linux From Scratch or Gentoo. I myself am also fan of Slackware, besides AntiX, and I used some scripts from Slackware to build my Crux install. And I use Paco (Package-Organiser), which is also great. Crux has specific features, like BSD-style init and a ports-system. Unfortunately there is no forum community

    But it is quite an interesting distro. In a short time I was able to build a swift and smooth system, and there are a lot of software packages available. __{{emoticon}}__ I think it's suitable for people wanting to have controll over their linuxdistro 'From Scratch', without all the compiling and recompiling!
