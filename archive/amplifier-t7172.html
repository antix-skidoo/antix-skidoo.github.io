Kogomo wrote: My laptop has low sound. I remember someone posting a script to act as an amplifier.
Some previous releases of antiX shipped with both an equalizer and a pre-amplifier available within AlsaMixer.  In recent releases this no longer the case.  Either of them may be made available, but not both of them concurrently.

Presently antiX ships with a system-wide equalizer available which can be accessed via the Control Centre (CC).  This will be deactivated, but remain visible in the CC when enabling the preamp.

The steps to activate the preamp are straightforward, but must be done manually.


To Enable the Preamp

Switch the system-wide configuration of AlsaMixer
Rename /etc/asound.conf to /etc/asound.conf.EQUALIZER
Copy /etc/asound.conf.PREAMP to /etc/asound.conf


Enable AlsaMixer to use the configuration for the preamp
Open a terminal and input the following
service alsa-utils stop
service alsa-utils start


Add the preamp slider control to AlsaMixer
Control Centre-->Hardware-->Test Sound
Note: this is a one time only step.  The playing of a sound file at this stage automatically and permanently displays a slider control named Pre-Amp in AlsaMixer


Adjust AlsaMixer to your preferred levels
Control Centre-->Hardware-->Adjust Mixer
Note: the Pre-Amp slider is usually located as the last slider on the right hand side of the AlsaMixer window
Adjust the slider as desired
Note: setting the Pre-Amp slider to an excessively high level can distort the sound and might damage the loudspeakers.  It is safer to use a low Pre-Amp level, and adjust the overall volume level via the other sliders.
