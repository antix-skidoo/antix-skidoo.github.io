Capitalism and socialism are not political systems but, rather, economic philosophies. They are nothing more (or less ) than concepts, ideas, theories until they are put into practice in a society at which point they become political.

In theory, capitalism offers economic freedoms, incentives, and individual opportunity. Socialism, in theory, promises economic security and economic & social equity.

In practice, however, capitalism inevitably becomes driven by greed while socialism promotes economic dependency and places the individual at the"mercy" of the state (a concept to which capitalism is not completely foreign, as well).

My point is this. Neither system is perfect and both have their pros and cons. Neither system can be singularly blamed for life's woes nor can either system be offered as the solution. While both systems work in theory they are both, in practice, susceptible to human corruption.

The problem is not social, political, or economic. The problem is spiritual. Human nature is inherently corrupt and, apart from divine intervention, will remain so. There is no social, political, or economic fix for that. The problem is sin. The solution is Jesus Christ.
